Exploring Google Earth Engine, Machine Learning, and GIS for Land Use Land Cover Change Detection in the Federal Capital Territory, Abuja, between 2014 and 2023

This study aims to visualize various land use land cover (LULC) classes, estimate the net change in LULC types between the years 2014 and 2023, and use transition mapping to track LULC transitions within waterbody, vegetation, bareland, and buildup to better understand how land use types change from one another.

Geospatial Assessment of Land Use/ Land Cover of the Federal Capital Territory: A Case Study for The Year 2050

This study presents the geospa al assessment of LULCC in the FCT (Federal Capital Territory) of Nigeria over a 35-year period, selecting the following years: 1987, 2002, 2011, and 2022, based on low cloud cover using remote sensing imagery and GIS techniques. By incorporating integrated cellular automata (CA)-Markov module from IDRISI-TerrSet soware, the year 2050 was predicted.

Monitoring and Mapping of Atmospheric Concentration of Carbon Monoxide, Sulphur Dioxide, and Nitrogen Dioxide from 2019 - 2022 in Benin City, Southern Nigeria

This study presents a comprehensive monitoring and mapping assessment of the atmospheric concentrations of carbon monoxide (CO), sulphur dioxide (SO2), and nitrogen dioxide (NO2) from 2019 - 2022 in Benin City, Southern Nigeria using Google Earth Engine (GEE) and Sentinel 5P (S5P) TROPOMI satellite data.
