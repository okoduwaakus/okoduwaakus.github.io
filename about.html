Biography

I am Akus Kingsley Okoduwa, a GIS and Remote Sensing researcher with a strong academic foundation in Environmental Management and Toxicology. I am currently pursuing an MSc in GIS and Environmental Sustainability at the University of Benin, Nigeria. My work involves using geospatial technologies and in-situ data to address pressing environmental issues across sub-Saharan Africa.

As a Research Assistant to Dr. Chika F. Amaechi and Prof. Alex A. Enuneku, I have mentored undergraduate and graduate students in remote sensing and GIS applications. My academic journey and field experience have led to over 20 peer-reviewed publications covering land use/land cover change, air quality, drought and flood assessment, and climate monitoring using platforms like Google Earth Engine, ArcGIS, and Python.

My long-term goal is to lead a research consultancy that supports evidence-based environmental policymaking and fosters sustainable community development through geospatial intelligence.
